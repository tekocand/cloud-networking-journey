# Trading Data Sources

## Philosophy
Use **X/Twitter as the primary narrative source**, but not as the only truth source.

The trading system should use:
- X for fast narrative discovery
- secondary sources for confirmation
- journal/history for self-calibration

## Source Priority
### Tier 1 — Narrative Discovery
Use these first for what the market is talking about:
- X / Twitter
- curated lists on X
- key accounts
- search terms / cashtags / themes

### Tier 2 — Validation
Use these to confirm or reject what X is saying:
- market/news websites
- exchange or market dashboards
- TradingView charts
- economic calendar
- earnings calendar
- protocol / project announcements
- ETF / macro decision calendars

### Tier 3 — Internal Evidence
Use these to compare public narrative against your actual results:
- your trade journal
- your setup history
- your playbook definitions
- your weekly review notes

## Source Roles
### X / Twitter
Best for:
- emerging themes
- crowd attention
- early narrative shifts
- repeated asset mentions
- sentiment acceleration

Weaknesses:
- noisy
- reflexive
- manipulated at times
- engagement can fake importance

### News / Calendar / Announcements
Best for:
- validation of catalysts
- timing of risk events
- checking whether narrative has real event support

### Charts / Market Data
Best for:
- confirming whether narrative has translated into price behavior
- checking if rotation is real
- checking whether setup is extended or still actionable

### Journal / Playbook
Best for:
- knowing whether a popular theme actually fits your edge
- preventing narrative-chasing

## Hard Rule
No X narrative should become a trade idea without validation from:
1. chart structure
2. risk clarity
3. catalyst / event context when relevant
